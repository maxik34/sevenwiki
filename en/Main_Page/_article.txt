<h1>Welcome to the Seven Wiki</h1>
<p>This is an independent fan-project dedicated to the detailed study of Windows 7. It contains technical details about the system's design, modifications, and history.</p>

<p>The project is still under development, and many elements on the site are just stubs.</p>

<h2>Main (future) sections</h2>
<ul>
	<li><b>System:</b> NT 6.1 kernel architecture, registry operations, and system calls.</li>
	<li><b>Interface:</b> Aero Glass, Explorer customization, and visual styles.</li>
	<li><b>Deployment:</b> Working with .WIM images, bootloaders, and installation automation.</li>
	<li><b>Components:</b> Overview of built-in applications and libraries (DLLs).</li>
</ul>

<h2>Command Line: File Checker (SFC)</h2>
<dl>
	<dt>/SCANNOW</dt>
	<dd>Checks the integrity of all protected system files and restores them if possible.</dd>
	<dd>Requires administrator rights and may take 5 to 20 minutes depending on disk speed.</dd>

	<dt>/VERIFYONLY</dt>
	<dd>Only integrity check without performing any recovery operations.</dd>

	<dt>/OFFBOOTDIR</dt>
	<dd>Specifies the drive letter for offline recovery (used in WinPE environment).</dd>
</dl>

<h2>Registry Data Types</h2>
<dl>
	<dt>REG_SZ</dt>
	<dd>Fixed length text string.</dd>
	
	<dt>REG_DWORD</dt>
	<dd>A 32-bit number. Most often used for logical parameters (0 or 1).</dd>
	
	<dt>REG_EXPAND_SZ</dt>
	<dd>An expandable string variable containing environment variables, such as <code>%SystemRoot%</code>.</dd>
</dl>
